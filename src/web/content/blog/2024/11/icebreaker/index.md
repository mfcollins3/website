A number of years ago, back before my son, my first child, was born, I joined a local [Toastmasters](https://www.toastmasters.org/) club in my home city. I was not horrible at public speaking, but I would get very nervous and quite honestly had bad time management and organization for speaking. The very first project, or speech, that each new Toastmasters member does is called the __Ice Breaker__ speech. The goal is to get up and speak for four to six minutes and basically survive. The Ice Breaker speech is intended to teach new members that public speaking is nothing to be afraid of, and by doing your Ice Breaker speech, you have now completed the hardest speech of your life. The rest is down hill from there.

Just as it was with public speaking, blogging can be quite a scary and challenging activity. What do I want to share? If I write it will people come? Will people disagree with what I write on my blog? Will I be made fun of? Will my blog scare away potential employers? There's a lot of anxiety that goes into starting a blog. But much like making a speech, writing that first blog post and publishing it is going to be the hardest blog post that I write. So here I am, tackling this challenge.

I come to this new blog and website after many previous attempts. There's a reason why I failed, which I will go into in a later post. But I have begun again because I choose not to be defined by those previous failures. I learned a lot about myself in those previous incarnations, and now I begin again and hope to yet again avoid failure.

Professionally, I am a software developer, software architect, leader, and manager with thirty years of professional software development experience. According to the [Stack Overflow 2024 Survey](https://survey.stackoverflow.co/2024/professional-developers#1-years-of-professional-work-experience), I am part of 5.8% of the industry that have kept my career alive for 30 years or longer. It's an achievement that I am proud of, and no, it has not been easy. But when I started, there were not a lot of us, so I am not surprised that I'm in a very small minority in terms of career.

I love programming and developing software. I'm not really a fan of the term _software engineer_. I much more prefer the term _software developer_ to describe my career. I build stuff, not necessary engineer it. I feel that my career and role is closer to that of a writer than of an engineer, as I like to spend my day sitting in front of a keyboard writing, and the result of my writing is a creative achievement that does wonderful things for people and solves some sort of problem.

I am a __polyglot__ programmer, which means that I know how to program in multiple languages. The primary programming languages that I use on a daily basis are:

In future posts to my blog, I will be showing off a lot of these different programming languages and teching you how to do some wonderful things in them.

I love building all types of applications. The last few years, I have specialized in building mobile applications with cloud backends. I work mostly on the Apple platforms (iOS, iPadOS), but also have learned how to build applications for Android as well using both Java and Kotlin. I have produced cloud native applications for Amazon Web Services, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud Platform. My current favorite, after a long time of persuasion, is Microsoft Azure. It took a few years, but the crew at Microsoft finally won me over for their development experience.

I am currently employed as a Senior Director for Application Innovation at [Neudesic](https://www.neudesic.com), a national consulting firm that is now a part of [IBM](https://www.ibm.com). I manage a large team of software development consultants that specialize in building custom cloud native, web, and mobile applications for customers. As the director, I am responsible for hiring and retaining staff, training my team of the latest and greatest technologies and tools, communicating my technical vision to my team and to the market that I serve, and ensuring that my team is delivering the best software that they can. I have been with Neudesic since May 2008 and have had quite the career roller coaster ride while working for them.

In my personal life, I live in Surprise, Arizona, with my wife Mea and our three children: Michael IV, Alexandra, and Audrey. I try to be very involved in my children's lives and I am very proud every day to see them continue to grow, learn, and achieve. My son Michael is very much into basketball. Alexandra is an amazing student, is extremely brilliant, and loves to read. Audrey is a very passionnate dancer and loves to perform on stage in front of an audience. My wife Mea is of Indian descent. She was adopted by an American couple from an orphanage in Calcutta, India as a ten week old baby and has lived in the United States ever since. We have been married for 18 years and will have been together for 20 years in March 2025.

When I am not programming, you will find me exploring my second love: cooking. I love to cook. I started off with baking, but over the last few years have expanded into different kinds of foods and main courses. As I write this article, a few days ago I made Thanksgiving dinner for my family. On the menu as a roasted turkey, apple cranberry stuffing, mashed potatoes with gravy, green beans, orange sweet potatoes, fresh baked bread, and a prime rib that I smoked in the Pit Boss pellet grill that I have in my back yard. That was quite the amazing dinner. I will be posting some recipes and experiments that I do in the kitchen from time to time to share what recipes that I love and hope you will try out too.

The primary focus of my blog will be technical in nature with a bit of career advice from my thirty-plus-year career. I will be talking about programs or applications that I am writing, technologies that I find very cool, or ideas that I have that I want to explore. I am sharing on my blog both to educate you, but also because writing about some of these topics helps me to organize and analyze my thoughts. I will share with you the good and the bad, the mistakes and the successes, the failures and the achievements. I will not always build things that work. I will not always do things the _right way_. Sometimes, I will do things the wrong way because I am learning and want to explore, and I'm ok with that.

I would love to interact with others to get your opinions. Feel free to contact me over email, or through comments on the blog posts. I always welcome constructive criticism. If you are here just because you want to troll on other programmers and you have proclaimed yourself one of the mythical and legendary 10X programmers, this probably isn't the right place on the Internet for you to be. But for everyone else, I welcome you. I hope to entertain and educate you. I hope that you learn from my ideas and adventures, and that I am able to help you in small or large ways.

Welcome to my website and blog! I can't wait to see where we will be going together.
